5 Fun Fidget Spinner Tricks to Try

Are you tired of the same old fidget spinner tricks? Here are 5 fun and unique tricks to try out:

  • 1. The "Around the World" trick: spin your fidget spinner on one finger and then pass it to the other hand without stopping it.
  • 2. The "Laser Pointer" trick: spin your fidget spinner on a flat surface and use your other hand to trace patterns in the air with the reflection.
  • 3. The "Butterfly" trick: spin two fidget spinners at the same time, one in each hand, and cross your arms to create a butterfly shape.
  • 4. The "Spinner Stack" trick: stack multiple fidget spinners on top of each other and see how high you can go before they fall.
  • 5. The "Fidget Spinner Ballet" trick: spin your fidget spinner on one finger and use your other hand to choreograph a mini-dance routine.

The History of Fidget Spinners

Fidget spinners have exploded in popularity in recent years, but where did they come from?

Fidget spinners were originally designed as a tool to help people with anxiety, ADHD, and other conditions that cause fidgeting. They were patented in 1993 by Catherine Hettinger, who hoped to sell them as a calming toy for children. However, the fidget spinner didn't gain widespread popularity until 2017, when they became a viral trend on social media and caught the attention of mainstream toy companies.

Today, fidget spinners come in a wide variety of shapes, sizes, and materials, and are enjoyed by people of all ages as a fun and satisfying distraction.
